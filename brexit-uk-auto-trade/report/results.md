# Results

Run `python run_build_data.py` then `python run_analysis.py` to regenerate these numbers.

## UK production, 2022 (official, SMMT 2023)

775,014 cars, 101,600 commercial vehicles, 1.5 million engines. 182,000 people work in manufacturing, 780,000 across the sector. The sector brings in £77 billion, a tenth of all UK exports.

## UK-EU trade index, 2016-2023 (reconstructed)

The index sits near 100 from 2016 through 2020, drops to 52 in 2021, then climbs to 80 by 2023. That is a 20-point gap still open three years after the drop. The reconstruction follows the ONS's own description of the shape: a fall of roughly half, then a partial, gradual recovery.

## LSE-listed automotive companies index, 2004-2021 (reconstructed)

The index falls from 100 in 2004 to 65 by 2021, with the steepest drop after 2016. That matches the direction reported by the London Stock Exchange: fewer listed automotive and parts companies once the referendum result was known.

## Global EV stock index, 2005-2022 (reconstructed)

The index grows from 1 in 2005 to 520 by 2022, an exponential curve. Growth accelerates hardest after 2018, consistent with the global EV adoption curve described in the source material.

## Post-Brexit trade agreements

38 active free trade agreements signed after Brexit. The UK-Japan CEPA, signed 31 December 2020, keeps car tariffs at zero between the two countries.
